Gukbap (국밥) is a beloved Korean dish that combines **rice (`bap`)** in hot **soup (`guk`)**. It's comfort food at its finest — warm, savory, and deeply satisfying.

## What Is Gukbap?

At its simplest, gukbap is **rice served in or alongside a hot broth**. But that simple definition barely scratches the surface. Across Korea, dozens of regional variations have developed, each with its own unique broth, toppings, and traditions.

The most famous styles include:

- **Dwaeji-gukbap (돼지국밥)** — Pork soup rice, the legendary Busan specialty
- **Kongnamul-gukbap (콩나물국밥)** — Bean sprout soup rice, a Jeonju classic
- **Sundae-gukbap (순대국밥)** — Blood sausage soup rice
- **Sogogi-gukbap (소고기국밥)** — Beef soup rice
- **Seolleongtang (설렁탕)** — Milky ox bone soup (technically a *tang*, but closely related)

## Why Gukbap Is Having a Moment

Gukbap is experiencing a surge in global popularity:

- **Baemin** (Korea's #1 food delivery platform) named **"New Wave Gukbap"** one of the top three food trends of 2025
- **NYT Cooking** published a featured gukbap recipe in January 2025
- **Netflix's *Culinary Class Wars*** (2024) spotlighted Okdongsik, a famous gukbap restaurant
- Foreign tourists in Korea ranked gukbap **7th among their favorite foods** in 2024 — up from nowhere in 2022
- K-pop idols like **Stray Kids** have been seen enjoying gukbap on variety shows
