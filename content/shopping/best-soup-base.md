Making gukbap from scratch is rewarding, but sometimes you need a shortcut. These Korean soup base products deliver authentic flavor with minimal effort — perfect for weeknight gukbap cravings.

## Top Picks

### 1. Beksul Pork Bone Soup Base (돼지고기육수)

The closest thing to an authentic Busan-style dwaeji gukbap broth in packet form. Beksul is a trusted Korean brand, and their pork bone soup base produces a rich, milky broth that rivals hours of simmering.

- **Best for:** Dwaeji gukbap, pork soup rice
- **Flavor:** Rich, savory, slightly creamy
- **Convenience:** Just add water and boil

### 2. ChoripDong Pork Bone Broth Concentrate

A liquid concentrate that's even closer to the real thing. ChoripDong specializes in Korean restaurant-quality broths, and this one is a favorite among home cooks.

- **Best for:** Dwaeji gukbap, seolleongtang
- **Flavor:** Deep, collagen-rich, authentic
- **Convenience:** 1 tbsp per cup of water

### 3. Ottogi Beef Bone Soup Base (사골곰탕)

Ottogi's beef bone soup base is a pantry staple in Korean households. It's the easiest way to make a convincing seolleongtang-style broth at home.

- **Best for:** Seolleongtang, sogogi gukbap
- **Flavor:** Mild, beefy, clean
- **Convenience:** One packet makes 4-5 cups of broth

### 4. Ottogi Anchovy Soup Base (멸치다시마)

For kongnamul gukbap and other light soups, this anchovy and kelp soup base is unbeatable. It provides the clean, umami-rich foundation that Jeonju-style bean sprout soup needs.

- **Best for:** Kongnamul gukbap, vegetable soups
- **Flavor:** Clean, briny, umami
- **Convenience:** Teabag-style packets

### 5. Sempio Doenjang (Soybean Paste)

While not a soup base per se, *doenjang* is the foundation of many Korean soups — including vegetarian gukbap. Sempio is Korea's most trusted brand.

- **Best for:** Vegetarian gukbap, doenjang-jjigae
- **Flavor:** Deep, funky, savory
- **Convenience:** Stays fresh in the fridge for months

| Milky beef broth for seolleongtang | Ottogi Beef Bone Base |
| Light broth for kongnamul gukbap | Ottogi Anchovy Base |
| Plant-based gukbap | Sempio Doenjang + dashima |

## Pro Tips

- **Mix bases** — Combine pork bone base + a dash of anchovy base for more complex flavor
- **Add fresh aromatics** — Even with a base, adding fresh garlic, ginger, and scallions makes a big difference
- **Keep them stocked** — These bases have long shelf lives and make impromptu gukbap possible anytime
